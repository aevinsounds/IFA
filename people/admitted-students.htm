Admitted Student Resources

Accepting the Offer of Admission

We are very pleased that you have made the decision to join us. To officially accept the offer, you must use our “Accept/Decline” web page, including paying your $250 (U.S.) enrollment deposit. You must make your official reply by the deadline date stated in your offer of admission. To do this, follow these simple instructions:

✓ Activate your NYU NetID and create your password. You have been assigned a NetID, included in your admission letter. Your NetID is used to sign in to systems at NYU. To activate this NetID and set your password, go to the NYU Information Technology Services (ITS) Start Page. Enter your NetID, click "start," and follow the online instructions. To complete the process, you will also need your date of birth and your NYU University ID number. Your NYU University ID is also in your admission letter.

✓ Sign-in to NYU Home. Go to NYU Home. NYU Home is a portal of entry to access NYU services online. Your username is your NetID, and your password is the one you have just created.

✓ Officially accept your offer of admission and pay the enrollment deposit by the deadline date stated in your offer of admission. To do so, click on "Applicants" within NYUAlbert, followed by "Application Status." Please then follow the online reply process by following the instructions on the Accept/Decline Admission pages.

PLEASE NOTE: If you do not see a box with the words "Admissions Offer" on the Application Status page, please wait 24 hours and try again. If you still have a problem, contact gsas.admissions@nyu.edu.

Instructions for International Admitted Students

New York University has one of the largest international student populations in the United States and we are proud to continue this tradition of a culturally diverse and international community. Obtaining your visa is a crucial part of your graduate school preparation. The following information will help you with this process.

1. Read our International Student Reference Guide
2. Read the instructions about completing the I-20 / DS-2019 application on the Office of Global Services website.
3. All questions about the I-20 / DS-2019 should be directed to newinternationalstudents@nyu.edu

Tuition

The entire MA program is 40 credits or 10 courses (4 credits per course).  You should calculate the total cost of the program based on 40 units or credits.  You can look on NYU's Bursar website IFA Tuition for the current IFA rate but it is subject to change for the upcoming academic year.
